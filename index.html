The 4-Stage Legal Journey

1

Academic Foundation (Pre-LLB)

Students from the National Curriculum (HSC) usually begin with the **Certificate of Higher Education (CertHE) in Common Law**. A-Level students (with 2 Cs or better) may apply for direct 1st-year entry.

  • Duration: 1 Year
  • Qualification: Level 4 FHEQ
2

The LLB (Hons) Degree

Study locally in Dhaka via Recognized Teaching Centers of the **University of London** or **Hertfordshire**. You will cover 9-12 core modules including Criminal, Contract, Tort, and Trust Law.

Note: To practice in the UK, your degree must be a "Qualifying Law Degree" (QLD).
3

Professional Training (The BTC)

Post-graduation, students move to the UK for 1 year to complete the **Bar Transfer Course (BTC)** or BPC. You must also join one of the four **Inns of Court** (Lincoln's, Gray's, Inner Temple, or Outer Temple).

4

Call to the Bar & BTT

Upon passing the Bar, you are a "Barrister-at-Law". To practice in the High Court of Bangladesh, you then sit for the **Bar Transfer Test (BTT)** conducted by the Bangladesh Bar Council.

Entry Requirements

Academic (HSC)

Minimum GPA 3.5 in HSC. Must enroll via CertHE Common Law route initially.

Academic (A-Levels)

Minimum 2 full A-Levels with Grade C. Direct entry into Year 1 of LLB.

Language Proficiency

IELTS Academic score of 6.5 overall (no band less than 6.0) for BTC applications.
